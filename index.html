About the System

Smart Breeze is an advanced air conditioning solution designed to intelligently optimize energy efficiency while enhancing occupant comfort. By leveraging cutting-edge technologies such as machine learning, IoT, and computer vision, SmartBreeze autonomously adjusts temperature, airflow, and humidity in real-time based on occupancy and environmental data. Unlike traditional AC systems that rely on manual inputs, Smart Breeze continuously monitors and adapts to the conditions of the room, ensuring optimal comfort and minimizing energy consumption. With its affordable yet highly effective design, Smart Breeze is ideal for both commercial and residential settings, offering a cost-effective way to reduce energy waste while maintaining a pleasant indoor environment.

Automatic Temperature Adjustment

The system automatically adjusts the room temperature to maintain optimal comfort by considering factors like occupancy and environmental conditions, without the need for manual input.

Occupancy-Based Shutter Management

The AC’s airflow is directed based on where people are in the room, ensuring efficient cooling by focusing air where it's needed most, reducing energy waste.

Adaptive AC Unit Control

The system intelligently determines how many AC units should be active, adjusting in real-time based on the number of occupants, time of day, and room conditions.

Self-Adjusting Humidity Control

The system monitors humidity levels and adjusts them automatically to maintain a comfortable and healthy indoor environment.

Electricity Usage Forecasting

By analyzing patterns in room usage and environmental conditions, the system predicts future electricity consumption, helping with better energy management and cost control.

LITERATURE SURVEY

In recent years, there has been a growing interest in optimizing air conditioning systems for both energy efficiency and user comfort, particularly in warm climates. Many studies have explored various methods to enhance the efficiency of AC systems by leveraging machine learning, IoT, and advanced sensors.

Several research efforts have focused on occupancy-driven air conditioning systems. Studies have shown that incorporating occupancy detection can significantly reduce energy consumption by ensuring that AC units operate only when needed. Traditional systems that rely on infrared sensors or high-resolution cameras have proven effective, but their high costs make them impractical for many middle-income regions. More affordable solutions, such as using RGB cameras for occupancy detection, have been proposed to balance cost and efficiency.

Another important area of research has centered on real-time temperature and humidity control, with studies indicating that user comfort is influenced by both temperature and humidity, as well as airflow. Intelligent systems that automatically adjust these factors based on environmental data have been shown to enhance comfort while reducing energy consumption. Machine learning algorithms have been explored to optimize this balance by continuously learning and improving performance over time.

However, there is a noticeable gap in the literature regarding the adaptation of these systems for specific climates, particularly tropical regions like Sri Lanka. Most existing studies have focused on temperate climates, and there is limited research tailored to the unique environmental and economic challenges faced by tropical regions.

SmartBreeze addresses these gaps by incorporating local climate data and providing a cost-effective solution that integrates occupancy detection, adaptive AC control, real-time temperature and humidity regulation, and electricity usage forecasting. Through this approach, SmartBreeze aims to deliver both energy efficiency and enhanced comfort for users in tropical regions.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The growing demand for energy efficiency in air conditioning (AC) systems, particularly in commercial buildings, poses a significant challenge, especially in tropical regions like Sri Lanka, where AC usage is near-constant due to climate conditions. Traditional AC systems, which rely on manual inputs and lack real-time responsiveness to occupancy and environmental conditions, lead to substantial energy waste and reduced occupant comfort. Although advanced air conditioning solutions with object detection and smart controls exist, their high costs, driven by the use of expensive sensors, limit their adoption, particularly in middle-income countries.

Current systems fail to optimize energy consumption across multiple environmental parameters such as airflow, temperature, and humidity, leading to inefficiencies in energy use and occupant discomfort. Moreover, existing cost-effective alternatives do not provide sufficient autonomous control over these factors. There is a clear need for a more affordable, intelligent air conditioning system that can dynamically adapt to occupancy and environmental changes in real time, balancing energy efficiency with occupant comfort.

The research problem thus centers around developing a cost-effective, intelligent air conditioning system that autonomously adjusts temperature, airflow, and humidity in response to real-time data on occupancy and environmental conditions. This system must integrate machine learning, IoT, and computer vision technologies, utilizing simpler sensors such as RGB cameras for occupancy detection, to provide an accessible solution capable of optimizing both energy efficiency and occupant comfort, without incurring the high costs of current advanced systems.

RESEARCH GAP

This research addresses key gaps in existing literature by focusing on thermal comfort and energy efficiency within the context of Sri Lanka's unique climate, which has been underexplored in previous studies that primarily focus on foreign environments. By utilizing local data, our study provides a tailored solution to improve both thermal comfort and energy management specifically for Sri Lankan buildings. A critical aspect that has been overlooked in prior research is the impact of architectural features, such as room size and window configurations, on energy consumption and comfort levels. This research underscores the importance of these elements, offering new insights into how they influence indoor environments. Additionally, while previous studies have often examined temperature or humidity in isolation, our comprehensive approach integrates multiple factors—including temperature, humidity, and airflow direction—using occupancy patterns and environmental data to optimize comfort and energy use. These contributions deliver a localized, multi-factor solution that advances research in energy-efficient air conditioning systems for Sri Lanka.

MAIN OBJECTIVE

Our intelligent air conditioning system is designed to optimize energy efficiency and enhance occupant comfort by autonomously regulating temperature, airflow, and humidity in real time. Utilizing smart technologies such as IoT and computer vision, the system adapts to occupancy and environmental data, ensuring that energy is used efficiently without sacrificing comfort.

Specific Objectives
Determining the Optimal Number of Active AC Units
The system intelligently determines the ideal number of AC units to activate based on factors like occupancy, time of day, temperature, humidity, and room size. This ensures that only the necessary units are active, balancing energy efficiency with optimal comfort.

Adaptive AC Unit Activation Based on Occupancy Detection
By using motion detection and depth estimation technologies, the system monitors occupancy and adjusts AC unit operation in real time. It activates only the necessary units based on the location and number of occupants, providing cooling where needed while minimizing energy waste.

Dynamic AC Blade Adjustment for Targeted Cooling
The system adjusts the direction of AC blades based on occupancy and room layout. This targeted cooling ensures that airflow is directed toward areas with higher occupancy, enhancing comfort while reducing unnecessary energy usage.

Optimizing Room Comfort through Adaptive Temperature Control
The system automatically adjusts air conditioning settings to maintain the optimal room temperature. It recognizes daily temperature patterns and makes predictions, ensuring energy-efficient operation while keeping the environment comfortable for occupants.

Effective Fan Speed Modification for Optimal Airflow
The system dynamically adjusts fan speed in response to factors such as occupancy, activity levels, temperature, and humidity. This optimization ensures that airflow is matched to current conditions, enhancing comfort and minimizing energy waste.

Predicting Optimal Indoor Humidity Levels
The system continuously monitors real-time data on temperature, occupancy, and external weather conditions to control and maintain ideal indoor humidity levels. This ensures a comfortable environment while preventing issues like excessive dryness or humidity, all while maximizing energy efficiency.

Forecasting AC Electricity Usage
The system includes a feature that forecasts air conditioning electricity consumption based on room size, occupancy, and time of day. This proactive approach helps in managing energy consumption more effectively, reducing costs while ensuring user comfort.

METHODOLOGY

The methodology for this research project focuses on developing an AI-enhanced HVAC system that optimizes energy efficiency and user comfort, specifically for Sri Lanka's tropical climate. The development of AC systems originated from the increasing demand to control the thermal comfort of indoor environments. Moreover, these systems were well known for their high energy consumption, which led to a pressing need for more intelligent and efficient solutions. The proposed methodology is organized into several stages to achieve energy efficiency while maintaining the optimal comfort level by optimizing several important parameters within air conditioning systems. During the proposed work, first of all by analyzing previous trends and all, decide how many numbers of ACs should be turned on in order to achieve energy efficiency while maintaining the optimal comfort level. Once after the number of Acs defines, which ACs should be turned on factor also takes into consideration. After deciding number of ACs and which ACs, now it’s time to focus on the important factors like airflow, temperature and humidity. Adjust the airflow based on the occupant presence, controlling room temperature based on several environmental factors and at the same time control the fan speed to adjust the room temperature for its ideal level and also control the relative humidity inside the space automatically without manual intervention, makes the proposed work unique and a sustainable solution.
